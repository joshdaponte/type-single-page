History of the font

Fraunces is a contemporary serif typeface designed by Flavia Zimbardi and Phaedra Charles. It started development in 2018 and was released in 2020 through Undercase Type. The design pulls from early 20th-century display typography, especially the bold and expressive serif styles seen in vintage advertising and editorial headlines. It is classified as a display serif, meant for larger applications where personality and impact matter most. Fraunces stands out for its variable font system, which allows designers to adjust axes like weight, optical size, softness, and wonk. These changes let the typeface shift from sharp and high-contrast to softer and more playful forms. It also supports a wide range of Latin-based languages, making it useful across many contexts. Fraunces balances historical influence with modern flexibility, which is why it works well in branding, web design, and editorial layouts.

Supported languages include:

English, French, Spanish, Portuguese, German, Italian, and many other Latin-based languages.
